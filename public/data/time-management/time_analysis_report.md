## Actionable Recommendations

1. **Implement a 25-minute timer rule.** Your productive sessions average under 2 minutes. Use a Pomodoro timer -- commit to 25 minutes of uninterrupted work before allowing any context switch. Even getting 4 of these per day would dramatically increase your deep-work count from the current 0.3/day.

2. **Front-load hard work to the morning.** Your data shows entertainment creeps from 15.3% in the morning to 16.8% by evening. Schedule your most cognitively demanding tasks before noon.

3. **Protect your peak hours (16:00, 20:00, 10:00).** These are your highest-focus hours. Block them as 'Do Not Disturb' -- no social media, no YouTube, no messaging.

4. **Watch out for Social Media** -- once you start, there's a 65.7% chance you stay. This is your stickiest distraction. Consider blocking it during work hours entirely, not just relying on willpower.

5. **Reduce your morning startup time.** You currently take 65.6 min on average from your first activity to first productive work. Prepare your work environment the night before -- leave your IDE open, have a task ready.

6. **Prioritize sleep for productivity.** Your data shows a positive correlation (0.60) between sleep and next-day productive output. Aim for >= 4.0h consistently.

7. **Create browser friction.** Use separate browser profiles: a 'Work' profile that blocks YouTube, Reddit, X/Twitter, and HiAnime; and a 'Personal' profile for after-hours use. This adds a speed bump to the Productive -> Entertainment drift.
